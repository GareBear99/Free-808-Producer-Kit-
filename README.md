## 🎛️ 808 Variant Guide

### Classic
Standard TR-808 sub bass character. Smooth pitch sweep from ~3.5× the root frequency, clean sine wave core with a subtle 2nd harmonic. Use this as your baseline — sits well in almost any mix.

### Punchy
Front-loaded transient energy with a noise click at the attack. Pitch sweeps fast and hard. Great for trap hi-hats that need the kick and 808 to breathe in the same space.

### Long
Extended 2.5s tails with a slow amplitude decay — designed for melodic trap where the 808 carries the bassline. Layered 3rd and 4th harmonics keep it present in the mids.

### Distorted
Soft-clip saturation (tanh) gives this 808 bark and grit without aliasing. Cuts through heavily compressed mixes. Best used for 808s doubling the main hook.

### Vintage
Modelled after the original Roland TR-808 bass drum circuit. Slight LFO wobble (5.5Hz) adds analog warmth. Harmonic layering at 2× and 3× gives it body.

### Sub
Pure sub, nothing more. Minimal harmonics, maximum low-end displacement. Built for rooms, cars, and speakers with proper woofers. Pair with a click layer if you need attack.

### Bright
Five harmonic layers (fundamental through 5th harmonic) make this 808 audible even on earbuds. Fast pitch sweep. Use this when your sub is getting lost in a dense mix.

## 🖥️ DAW Setup

### FL Studio
1. Open the **Sampler** channel
2. Drag any WAV into the sample slot
3. Set root key to match file name (e.g. `_C2` → C2)
4. Enable **"Pitch" knob** to keep pitch locked on playback

### Ableton Live
1. Drag WAV into a **Drum Rack** pad
2. Right-click pad → **Simpler**
3. Set **Root Key** in Simpler to match file name
4. Use **Clip Transpose** to pitch to your track key

### Logic Pro X
1. Add an **EXS24 / Sampler** instrument
2. Map each WAV to its corresponding MIDI note
3. Or drag directly into the **Audio Track** timeline

### MPC / Maschine
1. Load WAVs into your kit — assign each to a pad
2. Tune pads to root key using the pad's semitone offset
3. Use MIDI chord/note mode to trigger chromatic 808s live

## 💡 Production Tips

- **Sidechain** your 808 to the kick drum using a compressor — this is the #1 thing separating amateur from pro 808 sound.
- **Highpass your kick** at around 80Hz so the 808 owns the sub frequencies exclusively.
- **Pitch slides** — automate the pitch of a Long or Classic 808 over 4–8 steps for that melodic glide effect.
- **Layer** a Bright 808 under a Sub 808 at -6dB for best of both worlds: mono sub energy + harmonic presence.
- **Distorted 808s** pair best with parallel compression — keep a dry copy in the chain.
- **BPM-Synced** files work best as one-shots on beat 1 — let the tail breathe into beat 2 naturally.
